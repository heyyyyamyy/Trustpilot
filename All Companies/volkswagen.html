Ananya Reddy Chennai, India

As a product manager at Volkswagen Group, I have had the opportunity to work on some exciting projects. The company offers great benefits and a supportive work environment, but the work-life balance could be better. The pace can be overwhelming, especially when project deadlines overlap, making it hard to unwind.

Lian Zhang Shanghai, China

Working at Volkswagen Group in the engineering department has been a mixed experience. The innovation and technology are top-notch, and the teams are generally collaborative. However, the company’s decision-making process is slow, and it sometimes feels like there is too much red tape. It can be frustrating when you're trying to implement new ideas quickly.

Amina Al-Farsi Dubai, UAE

The work culture at Volkswagen Group is generally positive, and the company offers a range of benefits, including a solid health insurance package. However, I have found the work culture to be a bit overly competitive, which can sometimes lead to stress. Additionally, there is a lack of transparency in decision-making from higher management.

Priya Nair Mumbai, India

At Volkswagen, I work as a software developer and have had a largely positive experience. The company provides good opportunities for growth and skills development. The work culture is very fast-paced, which can be both exciting and stressful. One downside is that there can be a lack of recognition for individual efforts in team projects.

Ren Tanaka Tokyo, Japan

Volkswagen Group has a very organized structure with a focus on high-quality work. I’m happy with my role as a design engineer and appreciate the collaborative environment. However, the company can sometimes be too conservative with new ideas, and it can take longer than expected to make changes, which can be frustrating.

Fatima Al-Rashid Doha, Qatar

The team spirit at Volkswagen Group is fantastic, and there are good opportunities to move up the ranks. However, the company could improve work-life balance, especially for those working in high-pressure roles like mine in marketing. The hours can be long, and the expectations sometimes feel unrealistic.

Karthik Sundaram Bangalore, India

Volkswagen Group has a fantastic approach to career development and employee engagement. I have learned a lot during my time here in the IT department. However, the work culture can sometimes feel a bit too formal, and there are not enough casual social events to build camaraderie outside of work. That’s one area that could be improved.

Wei Zhang Beijing, China

Volkswagen Group provides a positive, forward-thinking work culture. The management is open to feedback, and there’s a strong emphasis on professional development. However, I feel there’s a lack of alignment between departments, which sometimes leads to confusion and duplicated efforts, hindering overall efficiency.

Khalid Al-Jaber Riyadh, Saudi Arabia

As a business analyst at Volkswagen Group, I find the work culture supportive and highly engaging. The flexibility to work remotely and the strong focus on work-life balance are excellent. However, there is still room for improvement in the overall communication between global teams, which can sometimes be disjointed.
